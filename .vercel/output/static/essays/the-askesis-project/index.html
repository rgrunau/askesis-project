The Askesis Project

January 5, 2026 · 3 min read · Read on Substack ↗

What if we could use the gym to explore and elevate who you are on the inside as well as out? What can pushing for a PR in your bench press teach you about over coming your fears and insecurities? How can one develop themselves mentally and physically without tipping into narcissism or theoretical masturbation? These are the questions I’ve spent a lot of time thinking about and trying to test and enact over the last sixteen years.

I’ve always yearned for a framework of self improvement that goes beyond just trying to “do better”. Now, after a two month coaching engagement with Micheal Blevins I feel like I have better understanding of the mechanics of change and a new direction for self exploration and writing. With this new direction comes new thoughts, which gives rise to new subjects to write about and explore. With that in mind, I’ve changed the name of this newsletter to The Askesis Project.

In its original meaning, Askesis is greek for physical and mental training. Since the Enlightenment we (in the West at least) have move toward a notion that the mind and body are nearly two separate entities that require a different set of stimuli to learn and grow, but this hasn’t always been the case. Nearly every ancient wisdom tradition that has survived the previous centuries sought to align mind and body. The Vedic Tradition has yoga, Taoism has Tai Chi and Kung Fu, and Greek philosophy schools were a mix of wrestling academies and institutions of higher purpose/learning. We are systems, where thoughts, emotions, and movement come together to shape our experience.

While I am not wise enough, nor arrogant enough to claim that I now know the way, that doesn’t change that my aim here will be to work toward what a physical philosophy might look like when practiced by someone who is essentially a novice. The Askesis Project is my attempt to bring together a variety of seemingly unrelated pursuits and theories into an integrated path of self improvement.

As I’ve said in past posts. I’ve tried this sort of radical change in the past, but Micheal’s mentorship in this area helped me see how I can forge my own path toward this integrated approach and how to elevate the gym up to the same pedestal as those spiritual pursuits listed above.

This is the ground I want to explore with this project. The integration of the body and mind with the aim of find a new way to steer the ship of life. For me, writing has always been the best way to process my day to day experience and the Askesis project will be the document of my attempt at self transformation. Perhaps the steps I’ve made toward living a better life can help someone else who struggling to live their best life.
