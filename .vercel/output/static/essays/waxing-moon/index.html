Waxing Moon

December 4, 2024 · 1 min read · Read on Substack ↗

Wash Your Bowls

Today’s post didn’t work out. I guess it needs to bake a little longer. This is what came out instead. Sometimes our realizations shallow and short lived, but beautiful all the same. I promise not to make anymore attempts at poetry.

Thanks for reading Wash Your Bowls! This post is public so feel free to share it.

Share

When a person attains realization, it is like the moon’s reflection in water. The moon never becomes wet; the water is never disturbed. — Eihei Dogen Zenji

Last night, while dinner was in the oven

I looked through the kitchen window and saw

A sliver of the waxing moon just above the last

Orange of the days’ light

I thought of Dogen’s lines bout the moon and became

Big; drained of wanting and fear.

Till it was time to help with the homework.
