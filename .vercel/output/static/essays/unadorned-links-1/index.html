Welcome to the first edition of unadorned links. This weekly post will be a review of things that caught my attention the last (couple) weeks.

I enjoyed this David Brooks column and it made me think a little more about my tendency to rage, my difficulties leading with love and I found the line about humility hit me pretty hard, and made me realize my general lack of humility. Henry Shukman and Sam Harris

I’ve listened to this conversation between Sam Harris and Henry Shukman on the Waking Up app (the link below should allow you to listen to the Q & A without having to sign up. That said, I think Waking Up is well worth the cost, and if you can’t afford a subscription, they provide scholarships that aren’t means tested. Henry Shukman is a great teacher and I happen to also subscribe to his app “The Way” which I’ve been using since it was released several months ago.

Danny Barnes

Danny Barnes is musician and artist who live not to far outside of Seattle. I first heard Danny way back in the early 2000s during the height of my Bill Frisell obsession. I’ve also had the pleasure of seeing him live twice. Once with David and Sam Grisman in the Dawg Trio. And another time when he opened for the Della Mae. He plays a free solo set at the Jefferson County Library every month that I hope to checkout.

Here’s Danny playing his song Overdue with Joe K Walsh and Grant Gordy
I’m going to learn this song some day.

One Idea I’ll be considering this week.

An idea I’ll be thinking about this week also comes from Danny Barnes. I’m watching his course on Peghead nation and the quote jumped out at me.

”As a musician you want to play something that improves silence, because silence is pretty hard to beat”

I’m one of those people who has a hard time sitting in a quite room. Maybe my desire for near constant sound has to do with drowning out the constant ringing in my ears, or perhaps it has to do with being alone with my thoughts. This next week I’ll try to meter toward silence.
