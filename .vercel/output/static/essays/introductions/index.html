Introductions

November 25, 2024 · 3 min read · Read on Substack ↗

Hello there,

I’m Robert Grunau, a middle aged software engineer in Seattle Washington and welcome to the Wash your Bowls Substack. We’ll get to where the title comes from in a moment but first let me finish this hopefully brief introduction.

For years I maintained a Wordpress based blog where I wrote (mostly) about cycling and how I processed and lived my life through racing, and riding bicycles. At first I wrote because I felt compelled to write. Later, I had the idea to make my life as a writer and I fumbled my way though trying to do that. I got close, but ultimately gave up on that goal to become a father, and start trying to make money through a more stable and lucrative pursuit. Now, the compulsion to write has come back.

With that out of the way we can now address the title of this here chronicle.

The phrase “Wash your bowls” comes from the Zen classic The Gateless Gate. This collection of 48 Zen Koans was compiled by Wumen Huikai in 13th century China. “Joshu’s Wash your Bowls” is case number seven and it reads:

A monk asked Joshu in all earnestness, “I have just entered this monastery. I beg you, Master please give me instructions.” Joshu asked, “Have you eaten your rice gruel yet?” The mood answered, “Yes, I have.” Joshu said, “then wash your bowls”. The monk attained some realization. (emphasis mine).

As far as koans go this one seems like it is pretty easy to wrap your head around as it reads like anecdote your Uncle told you after cornering you for at grandma’s Thanksgiving dinner.

To be clear, attempting to use one’s reason to uncover the meaning of any one Koan would be like heading off in southerly direction when your destination is just a few steps north of where you currently reside. With that out of the way, allow me to miss the point entirely.

Joshu’s instructions to this earnest monk (monks are always asking these questions earnestly) is to do the next logical thing one should do after enjoying a meal — clean up. “Wash your bowls” is a mantra fit for simply doing the next thing that needs doing. As someone who likes to often times starts on steps three or four, I can use the reminder.

Then next thing is for me is to return to writing like I did in the past when I felt compelled to write about my experiences in the open. This writing too will be a reflection of that earlier blog. I’m going back to what I know how to talk about, physical and mental effort, the merging of the two, and how the flips of that coin affect our lives. I learned a lot about myself and how I bumbled through the world by writing back then. I hope to do that again. Maybe my bumblings will be helpful to you too.

Hey there, if you’ve made it this far perhaps you’d like to consider subscribing
